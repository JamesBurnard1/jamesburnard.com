Experience

Jamix

Remote | May 2025 - September 2025

Data Science Intern

At Jamix Inc., I worked remotely as a Data Science Intern from May 2025 to September 2025, where I developed a data-driven sales prospecting workflow for the higher education market. I built a Python ETL pipeline using pandas, requests, and fuzzywuzzy to integrate HubSpot CRM data, College Scorecard API data from 6,429+ institutions, and institutional datasets into a cleaned and filtered dataset of approximately 1,000 relevant schools. I then used this dataset to create Tableau dashboards that analyzed selected institutional variables and combined those findings with Euclidean distance scoring and network analysis to identify 75 high-fit sales prospects. Alongside this project, I managed daily HubSpot data operations by importing and updating institutional records to support cross-functional requests.

Quantitative Research Assistant in Economics

Amherst, MA | January 2025 - April 2025

Assisted Professor Nathan Meyers

As a Quantitative Research Assistant in Economics from January 2025 to April 2025, I assisted Professor Nathan Meyers with an economic research project analyzing the relationship between union decline and financial outcomes across U.S. industries. I built a research pipeline using Python, R, SQL, Excel, and scikit-learn to analyze 1,174 industry-year observations across 710 variables, focusing on shareholder dividends, taxation, and labor compensation. I also developed an interactive 3D Plotly visualization of 886 industry-year observations across 44 industries and 27 years, allowing users to explore industry clustering and structural economic change over time. In addition, I applied econometric techniques including Difference-in-Differences analysis, regression modeling, and correlation analysis to evaluate the effects of union decline across multiple economic outcomes.

Undergraduate Economics Research Assistant for EURA

Amherst, MA | February 2024 - May 2024

Assisted Professor Kevin Young

As an Undergraduate Economics Research Assistant for EURA from February 2024 to May 2024, I assisted Professor Kevin Young with a historical data digitization and social network analysis project. I built a Python OCR pipeline using Tesseract, pdf2image, pandas, and regex parsing to digitize more than 4,500 physical book pages into structured CSV and JSON files containing 53,131 research-grade records across eight historical book editions. I then transformed historical membership data into social network models using HTML and JavaScript, spanning 218 organizations and 42,700 membership links to uncover patterns of organizational connectivity and continuity from 1967 to 1989. I also developed validation workflows to identify OCR errors, duplicate entries, missing fields, and unknown organization codes, improving the reliability of the research dataset.
